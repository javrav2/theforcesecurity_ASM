## The Problem with Traditional Vulnerability Management

Traditional VM tools like Qualys, Rapid7, and Tenable are excellent at what they do: **scan known assets for known vulnerabilities**. You give them a list of IP ranges, they scan them, they report CVEs with CVSS scores.

But this approach has a fundamental blind spot: **you can only manage what you know about.**

In 2026, the average enterprise has:

- Thousands of forgotten S3 buckets with public ACLs
- Dozens of dev/staging environments exposed to the internet
- Shadow IT apps deployed outside of IT's control  
- M&A assets from acquired companies never properly inventoried
- Cloud-native services spun up by developers that bypass traditional change management

These are the assets that attackers find first — and they're invisible to traditional VM tools.

## What ASM Actually Means

Attack Surface Management is the **continuous discovery, inventory, and risk assessment of all internet-facing assets** from an attacker's perspective.

The key phrase is *from an attacker's perspective*. You start with what an adversary can see — domain names, IP ranges, certificate transparency logs, BGP announcements — and work inward to build your asset inventory.

### The ASM Workflow

```
External Discovery
    │
    ├── DNS enumeration (subfinder, amass)
    ├── Certificate transparency (crt.sh, certstream)
    ├── BGP/IP intelligence (RIPE, Team Cymru)
    ├── Web crawling (gau, waybackurls)
    └── Cloud asset enumeration
    │
    ▼
Asset Attribution
    │
    ├── Confirm ownership
    ├── Identify technology stack
    └── Map to business unit/application
    │
    ▼
Continuous Monitoring
    │
    ├── Change detection
    ├── New asset alerts
    └── Service fingerprinting
    │
    ▼
Risk Assessment
    │
    ├── Vulnerability scanning (Nuclei)
    ├── Exposure scoring
    └── Prioritization
```

## Where They Complement Each Other

ASM and VM are not competing disciplines — they're **complementary layers of a defense-in-depth strategy**:

| Dimension | Traditional VM | ASM |
|-----------|---------------|-----|
| Asset input | Provided by you | Discovered autonomously |
| Perspective | Inside-out | Outside-in (attacker's view) |
| Cadence | Periodic (weekly/monthly) | Continuous |
| Primary focus | Known CVEs | Unknown/forgotten assets |
| Ideal for | Compliance, known infrastructure | Cloud-native, M&A, shadow IT |

## The 2026 Threat Landscape Makes ASM Non-Negotiable

Three trends have made ASM essential:

**1. Cloud expansion.** The average enterprise uses 4+ cloud providers. Ephemeral resources appear and disappear constantly.

**2. M&A velocity.** When you acquire a company, you acquire their attack surface — including the parts they didn't know about.

**3. AI-powered recon.** Attackers are using LLMs to automate reconnaissance at scale. The window between asset exposure and exploitation is shrinking.

## Getting Started with ASM

You don't need a $500K enterprise platform to get started:

1. **Start with your own domains** — run `subfinder -d yourdomain.com` and be prepared to be surprised
2. **Check certificate transparency** — `curl "https://crt.sh/?q=%.yourdomain.com&output=json" | jq`
3. **Audit your cloud storage** — look for public S3 buckets, Azure blobs, GCS buckets
4. **Run httpx on your discovered assets** — identify what's actually running
5. **Scan with Nuclei** — find the low-hanging fruit before attackers do

Our ASM platform runs these discovery workflows continuously and feeds findings into our prioritization engine (Aegis Oracle) which scores exploitability in context — not just CVSS. A critical CVE on a dev server that's not routable from the internet gets a different priority than the same CVE on your public-facing authentication endpoint.
